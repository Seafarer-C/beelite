## Spatial

### What it is
Spatial is a notes app embedded into a visual canvas. Made for keeping notes, scribbles, images, videos and web clips together in one space. Sort of like a digital shoebox.

Spatial is made for keeping notes, laying out graphics, photos, motion and animation, building a second brain with AI-friendly markdown exports, and managing daily life with the small stuff that runs the day. Items are laid out by location - spatially - which tend to be easier to remember than verbal structures. So you can find things faster and worry less about organization.

### Who it is for
It's for writers to have a calm space to write, with a unique way of organizing. It's for designers to collect visual reference material. It's for knowledge workers to build a second brain. And it's for anyone to help manage daily life.

### Why was it made
It's been the dream note taking app of mine for a long time. Writing has always been my go to way of kick off projects, to get my thoughts together. But as the number of notes grows, they get harder to keep track of. Some notes are small, some are big. Some are one-off scribbles, and others naturally go together with each other, or with a piece of media. A list off notes in a sidebar don't quite capture these nuanced relationships. Neither does tags, nor folders. Spatial is an exploration at using spatial memory for organization instead, which can feel a lot more natural and is also a very durable form of memory. Like how you still remember the layout of the house you grew up in, but might struggle to remember what was for lunch last tuesday. Compared to similar canvas based apps, the user experience is more fluid and natural, it supports video and has a mode for writing long form texts.

### Who made it
It was made by Tobias Renström, a digital product designer and builder who's also built a gym tracker called Dropset. It's been built in public on X (x.com/tbsrnstrm) and Threads (threads.com/tobberacing).

### Tech stack
It's a native app for macOS built with AppKit, designed in Figma. It's written in classic, manual imperative code, not vibe coded.
